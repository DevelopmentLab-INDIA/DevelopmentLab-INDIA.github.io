About DPLI

Evidence that stops at diagnosis is not enough

The Development Policy Lab India was founded on a simple conviction: most policy analysis tells you what is wrong. Very little of it tells you how to fix it. DPLI exists to close that gap.

We produce original research on governance and institutions, with a particular focus on implementation systems: the administrative capacity, human resources, digital infrastructure, and local governance mechanisms that determine whether public policy actually delivers results.

Our work is grounded in Indian realities but draws on international evidence. We apply rigorous analytical frameworks, build accessible public tools, and communicate findings in ways that are useful to policymakers, researchers, and development practitioners.

Founder

The person behind the work

DPLI is an independent initiative led by a researcher committed to closing the gap between policy analysis and policy action.

A
Anant Srivastava
Founder and Lead Policy Analyst, Development Policy Lab India
I work on one question: why do similar policies produce different outcomes across different institutions? The answer, I keep finding, is not in the policy. It is in the institution behind it. Across seven years of political consulting, civil society engagement, and independent research, that question has shaped everything I do. At DPLI, I build the analytical tools and produce the research to help answer it. My work spans governance diagnostics, live data analytics, and implementation-oriented policy frameworks grounded in evidence from India and South Asia and aligned with World Bank operational priorities on governance, institutions, and sustainable development.

Research themes

Five questions we are working to answer

Our research is organised around the governance questions that most directly shape development outcomes in India and comparable contexts.

01 / 05
State Capacity and Development
What determines whether good policies actually produce results? How does institutional capability shape the quality and reach of public service delivery?
02 / 05
Digital Public Infrastructure
How does DPI reshape the relationship between citizens and the state? What are the governance conditions under which digital systems generate genuine inclusion?
03 / 05
Health Systems as Economic Infrastructure
How do health system investments generate employment multipliers and macroeconomic returns beyond their direct health impact?
04 / 05
Women's Institutional Inclusion
Through what governance mechanisms do digital and administrative systems expand or constrain women's effective participation in economic and public life?
05 / 05
Implementation and Governance Reform
What practical frameworks help governments move from institutional diagnosis to measurable improvements in policy delivery and development outcomes?

DPLI Framework

The DPLI State Capacity Framework

A structured approach for understanding and strengthening the institutional capabilities required for effective governance and development outcomes.

The DISER methodology
D
DIAGNOSE
Define the challenge
Identify the nature, scale, and implications of the policy problem before proposing any solutions.
I
INVESTIGATE
Examine evidence
Identify root causes, review domestic and international experience, and draw actionable lessons.
S
SOLVE
Develop solutions
Design practical, evidence-based solutions tailored to the specific institutional context.
E
EXECUTE
Translate to action
Identify actions, timelines, stakeholders, and resource requirements for implementation.
R
REVIEW
Monitor and learn
Establish monitoring and evaluation mechanisms to assess progress and support continuous adaptation. The DPLI India State Governance Data Monitor provides the official government data infrastructure for this stage.

About this initiative

DPLI is an independent, unfunded research initiative. All publications are produced independently and are freely available.

Research is aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals and draws on frameworks developed by the World Bank, OECD, UNDP, and peer-reviewed governance literature. The DPLI State Governance Data Monitor displays official government data from NITI Aayog, DARPG, MoHFW, and UDISE+ to support evidence-based policy review.

If you are a researcher, policymaker, or development professional working in related areas and would like to discuss the work, please reach out via email or LinkedIn.

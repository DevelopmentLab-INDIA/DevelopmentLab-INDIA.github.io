Scope note: The ISCM Phase 1 covers 18 major Indian states selected on the basis of population size, availability of comparable data across all five capacity dimensions, and geographic and developmental diversity. Union territories and smaller states will be incorporated in subsequent editions. DPLI intends to expand coverage to all 28 states by 2028.
Note on index construction: The ISCM follows established composite index methodology used by the World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators, NITI Aayog SDG India Index, India Justice Report, and Public Affairs Index. Composite governance indices using publicly available government data require no government authorisation and do not constitute official government rankings. Full methodology documented in DPLI Methodology Note 01/2026.  |  Methodology & data sources: The India State Capacity Monitor (ISCM) is a composite index developed by the Development Policy Lab India (DPLI) using the DPLI State Capacity Framework. Dimension scores (Administrative, Human Resource, Digital, Local Governance, Data) are constructed from publicly available government data sources including the DARPG Good Governance Index 2021 (most recent published edition; GGI 2023 was cancelled by the Government of India; next edition expected December 2025 — scores will be updated upon release), MoHFW Health Dynamics of India 2022-23, UDISE+ 2023-24, Ministry of Panchayati Raj Annual Report 2023-24, and NITI Aayog Data Management & Governance Quality Index 2021. Where state-level data is unavailable or incomplete, DPLI expert assessment is applied. SDG index scores are sourced from NITI Aayog SDG India Index 2023-24 (published 12 July 2024; sdgindiaindex.niti.gov.in); remaining state SDG scores are DPLI estimates pending full verification. The implementation gap chart uses DPLI illustrative estimates based on published programme data and is indicative only. The ISCM is an analytical and policy tool and does not constitute an official government ranking. All scores and methodology are subject to revision as data improves.  |  Version 2.0 · June 2026
